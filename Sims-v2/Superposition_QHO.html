About This Simulation

This simulator demonstrates quantum superposition using the first two orthogonal energy eigenstates of the Quantum Harmonic Oscillator (QHO). The ground state $\psi_1$ and first excited state $\psi_2$ are:

$$\psi_1(x) = \left(\pi\sigma^2\right)^{-1/4}\exp\!\left[-\frac{x^2}{2\sigma^2}\right]$$ $$\psi_2(x) = \sqrt{2}\left(\pi\sigma^2\right)^{-1/4}\frac{x}{\sigma}\,\exp\!\left[-\frac{x^2}{2\sigma^2}\right]$$

These states are strictly orthogonal by parity ($\psi_1$ is even, $\psi_2$ is odd), guaranteeing $\langle\psi_1|\psi_2\rangle = 0$. This ensures unitary time evolution and exact probability conservation.

The full time-dependent superposition with QHO energies $E_1 = \tfrac{1}{2}\hbar\omega$ and $E_2 = \tfrac{3}{2}\hbar\omega$ is (with real $c_1, c_2 \geq 0$):

$$\Psi(x,t) = c_1\,\psi_1(x)\,e^{-iE_1 t/\hbar} + c_2\,\psi_2(x)\,e^{-iE_2 t/\hbar}$$

Because the eigenstates are orthonormal, normalization is the standard condition:

$$|c_1|^2 + |c_2|^2 = 1$$

The probability density oscillates at the beat frequency $\omega_{21} = (E_2 - E_1)/\hbar = \omega$. Since both coefficients are real and positive in this simulation:

$$|\Psi(x,t)|^2 = c_1^2|\psi_1|^2 + c_2^2|\psi_2|^2 + 2\,c_1 c_2\,\psi_1(x)\psi_2(x)\cos(\omega_{21}t)$$

The cross-term is x-dependent (positive for $x>0$, negative for $x<0$), causing the probability to slosh back and forth, mimicking classical oscillation. Total probability $\int|\Psi|^2 dx = 1$ is exactly conserved at all times. Natural units: $\hbar = m = 1$, so $\omega = 1/\sigma^2$.
